About Me

Hi! My name is Kate Witham, and I am a recent graduate of the University of Massachusetts Amherst with a degree in Political Science, a minor in English, and a certificate in Professional Writing and Technical Communication. Throughout my academic and professional experience, I have developed a strong interest in the way writing, design, research, and digital communication work together to create clear and meaningful content.

My work often combines creativity with organization. Whether I am writing professional documents, designing website layouts, creating branding materials, or researching complex topics, I enjoy taking information and making it more accessible, engaging, and visually effective. I am especially interested in user-focused design, digital storytelling, branding, grant writing, and technical communication.

During my time at UMass, I completed projects that allowed me to strengthen both my writing and design skills. I have worked with tools such as Figma, Adobe Illustrator, HTML, CSS, MadCap Flare, and Canva to create websites, prototypes, documentation, and visual materials. These experiences helped me become more comfortable adapting to new technologies while also thinking carefully about audience, accessibility, and usability.

I am someone who enjoys learning through hands-on projects and collaboration. I like being able to take an idea from its early planning stages and develop it into something polished and purposeful. My background in political science has also helped me become a stronger researcher and critical thinker, while my work in English and technical communication has strengthened my ability to write clearly for different audiences.

As I begin the next stage of my career, I am excited to continue building skills in writing, design, communications, and research. I am especially drawn to work that allows me to be creative while also helping organizations communicate their ideas more effectively.
