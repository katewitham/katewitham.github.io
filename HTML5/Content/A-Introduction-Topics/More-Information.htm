How to create and customize title cards

Title cards are text displayed on the screen to convey information to the watcher. In this way, they serve a similar purpose to chapters but may also introduce the video’s title, provide context, or separate individual segments of a video. ClipChamp’s Editor application can create and/or edit title cards to address different needs.

Create a title card in ClipChamp Editor

  1. Click on “Open editor.” ClipChamp’s built-in editing software opens.

  2. Click on “Text” on the left side menu. Editor opens a menu you can scroll through with different text options, including text with different styles.

  3. Select one option and drag it over to the time-line.

  4. Place the text above where you want it to appear in the video. A text box appears in the preview

  5. Click inside the text box in the preview.

  6. Type in whatever you want the text to say.

Change the font of the chapter card

  1. Click on the title from the timeline. The text property menu opens on the right where you can customize the text.

  2. Click on the dropdown arrow beside the font name. A scrollable dropdown menu opens with different font choices, including headings, subheadings, and body text.

  3. Select the desired font. After selecting a choice, the text will automatically change to the chosen font.

Adjust the alignment of the text

  1. Open the text property menu.

  2. Click on the “alignment” icon from the text property menu.

  3. Select from the three options: Left, Center, or Right The text within the box will follow the alignment you choose.

Change the font size/general size of the text

  1. Navigate to the preview and click on the text.

  2. Click and drag the corners of the text box to adjust its size.

Change the color of the text

  1. Navigate to the “Color” section of the text property menu. Depending on what text/text style you selected, the default color will be different.

  2. Click on the colored circle in the menu. Depending on the text/text style you selected, the color options offered will be different.

  3. Click on the desired color or type in a HEX value. The text will automatically change to the color you choose.
